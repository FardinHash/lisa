LIFE INSURANCE ELIGIBILITY AND UNDERWRITING

GENERAL ELIGIBILITY REQUIREMENTS

Age Requirements:
- Minimum age: 18 years (some policies allow 0-17 for child coverage)
- Maximum age for term life: typically 65-75 years
- Maximum age for whole life: typically 85 years
- Maximum age for final expense: typically 85 years
- No maximum age for guaranteed issue policies

Citizenship and Residency:
- Must be U.S. citizen or legal permanent resident
- Must have valid Social Security number or Tax ID
- Must reside in the United States
- Some exceptions for military personnel stationed abroad

Insurable Interest:
- Must have insurable interest in the insured person
- Self-insurance always permitted
- Spouses have insurable interest in each other
- Parents have insurable interest in children
- Business partners in each other for business purposes

UNDERWRITING PROCESS

Medical Underwriting:
The underwriting process evaluates risk factors to determine eligibility and premium rates.

Information Required:
- Personal information: age, gender, occupation, hobbies
- Medical history: current and past health conditions
- Family medical history: hereditary conditions
- Lifestyle factors: smoking, alcohol use, drug use
- Financial information: income, net worth, existing coverage
- Driving record: DUI, accidents, violations

Medical Examination:
For coverage above certain amounts, typically $250,000+:
- Height and weight measurement
- Blood pressure and pulse check
- Blood sample for testing
- Urine sample for testing
- EKG for older applicants or higher coverage
- Additional tests based on age and coverage amount

Underwriting Classes:
1. Preferred Plus/Elite: Best health, no tobacco, excellent medical history
2. Preferred: Very good health, no tobacco, good medical history
3. Standard Plus: Good health, no tobacco, minor health issues
4. Standard: Average health, may include tobacco users
5. Substandard/Rated: Health issues requiring higher premiums (Table ratings A-H)

HEALTH CONDITIONS AND IMPACT

Conditions Generally Accepted (may affect rates):
- Well-controlled diabetes
- High blood pressure (controlled with medication)
- High cholesterol (controlled)
- Asthma (mild to moderate)
- Sleep apnea (treated)
- Depression or anxiety (stable, treated)
- Previous cancer (5+ years in remission)
- Heart disease (stable, good function)

Conditions Requiring Specialized Underwriting:
- Active cancer or recent diagnosis (within 5 years)
- Severe heart disease or recent heart attack
- Kidney disease or dialysis
- Liver disease or cirrhosis
- HIV/AIDS
- Severe mental health conditions
- Drug or alcohol abuse history
- Multiple DUIs

Automatic Decline Conditions:
- Terminal illness with limited life expectancy
- Active drug or alcohol addiction
- Severe uncontrolled diabetes
- Recent heart attack (within 6-12 months)
- Certain high-risk occupations without specialized coverage

LIFESTYLE FACTORS

Tobacco Use:
- Smokers pay 2-3x higher premiums than non-smokers
- Includes cigarettes, cigars, chewing tobacco, vaping
- Must be tobacco-free for 12+ months to qualify for non-smoker rates
- Testing detects nicotine and cotinine in blood/urine
- Misrepresentation can void policy

Alcohol Use:
- Moderate drinking generally acceptable
- History of DUI affects eligibility and rates
- Multiple DUIs may result in decline or high rates
- Past alcohol abuse requires 2-5 years sobriety

Occupation:
High-risk occupations may affect rates or require specialized policies:
- Construction workers (especially high-rise)
- Pilots and aircraft crew
- Firefighters and police officers
- Mining and offshore oil workers
- Professional athletes
- Military personnel in combat zones

Hobbies and Activities:
High-risk activities may require exclusions or higher rates:
- Skydiving and BASE jumping
- Scuba diving (beyond recreational depths)
- Rock climbing and mountaineering
- Racing (cars, motorcycles, boats)
- Private pilot flying
- International travel to high-risk areas

SIMPLIFIED AND GUARANTEED ISSUE POLICIES

Simplified Issue Life Insurance:
- No medical exam required
- Application with health questions only
- Faster approval (often same day)
- Coverage typically up to $500,000
- Slightly higher premiums than fully underwritten
- Good for healthy individuals wanting convenience

Guaranteed Issue Life Insurance:
- No medical exam or health questions
- Acceptance guaranteed regardless of health
- Coverage typically $5,000 to $25,000
- Graded death benefit (first 2-3 years)
- Highest premiums
- Best for those with serious health conditions
- Common in final expense insurance

AGE-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS

Young Adults (18-30):
- Lowest premiums available
- Minimal health issues
- May not see immediate need
- Locking in low rates beneficial
- Typically qualify for preferred rates

Middle Age (30-50):
- Prime years for coverage
- Family protection needs peak
- Health issues may begin emerging
- Still qualify for good rates if healthy
- Important for mortgage and income protection

Older Adults (50-65):
- Higher premiums due to age
- Health conditions more common
- May face underwriting challenges
- Important for estate planning
- Consider permanent policies

Seniors (65+):
- Limited term life options
- Permanent policies still available
- Final expense insurance popular
- Guaranteed issue often needed
- Focus on funeral costs and estate

COVERAGE AMOUNT DETERMINATION

Income Replacement Method:
- 10-15x annual income
- Example: $75,000 income = $750,000 to $1,125,000 coverage
- Ensures family maintains standard of living

DIME Method:
- Debt: All outstanding debts (mortgage, loans, credit cards)
- Income: 10x annual income
- Mortgage: Remaining mortgage balance
- Education: Future education costs for children
Total these amounts for recommended coverage

Financial Obligations Method:
- Outstanding debts and loans
- Mortgage balance
- College education costs (estimated $100,000+ per child)
- 5-10 years of income replacement
- Final expenses ($15,000-$25,000)
- Emergency fund

Example Calculation:
Mortgage: $300,000
Student loans: $40,000
Credit cards: $10,000
College fund (2 kids): $200,000
Income replacement (10 years at $80,000): $800,000
Final expenses: $20,000
Total Recommended Coverage: $1,370,000

APPLICATION TIMELINE

Standard Process:
- Application submission: Day 1
- Medical exam scheduling: Days 2-7
- Medical exam completion: Days 7-14
- Underwriting review: Days 14-45
- Approval and policy issue: Days 45-60

Simplified Issue:
- Application submission: Day 1
- Underwriting review: Days 1-7
- Approval and policy issue: Days 7-14

Guaranteed Issue:
- Application submission: Day 1
- Immediate approval: Days 1-3
- Policy issue: Days 3-7

Factors Affecting Timeline:
- Medical records requests from doctors
- Additional medical tests required
- International travel or residency verification
- Financial documentation for high coverage amounts
- Clarification of health conditions
