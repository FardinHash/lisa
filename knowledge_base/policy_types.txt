LIFE INSURANCE POLICY TYPES AND COVERAGE

1. TERM LIFE INSURANCE

Term life insurance provides coverage for a specific period (term), typically ranging from 10 to 30 years. This is the most affordable type of life insurance.

Key Features:
- Pure death benefit protection with no cash value component
- Fixed premium payments throughout the term
- Coverage ends when the term expires unless renewed
- Available terms: 10, 15, 20, 25, or 30 years
- Premium amounts remain level during the term period

Benefits:
- Most affordable option for substantial coverage
- Simple and straightforward coverage
- Ideal for temporary needs like mortgage protection or income replacement during working years
- No medical exam options available for some policies
- Convertible to permanent insurance in many cases

Typical Coverage Amounts: $100,000 to $5,000,000

Best For:
- Young families needing affordable high coverage
- Mortgage or debt protection
- Income replacement during working years
- Business loan protection

2. WHOLE LIFE INSURANCE

Whole life insurance provides lifetime coverage with a cash value component that grows over time. Premiums and death benefits remain fixed.

Key Features:
- Permanent coverage lasting your entire lifetime
- Fixed premiums that never increase
- Guaranteed death benefit
- Cash value accumulation that grows tax-deferred
- Dividend payments from participating policies
- Ability to borrow against cash value

Benefits:
- Lifetime protection with guaranteed death benefit
- Builds cash value that can be accessed through loans or withdrawals
- Fixed premiums provide budget certainty
- Tax-deferred growth of cash value
- Estate planning tool
- Potential dividend income

Cash Value Growth:
- Accumulates slowly in early years
- Guaranteed minimum growth rate
- Typically 2-4% annual growth
- Tax-free loans available against cash value

Typical Coverage Amounts: $50,000 to $10,000,000

Best For:
- Long-term estate planning
- Lifetime coverage needs
- Building cash value for future needs
- High net worth individuals
- Funeral and final expense coverage

3. UNIVERSAL LIFE INSURANCE

Universal life insurance offers flexible premiums and death benefits with a cash value component that earns interest based on current market rates.

Key Features:
- Adjustable death benefit amounts
- Flexible premium payments
- Cash value earning market-based interest
- Ability to increase or decrease coverage
- Access to cash value through loans and withdrawals

Benefits:
- Premium payment flexibility
- Adjustable coverage amounts
- Potential for higher cash value growth than whole life
- Lower premiums than whole life initially
- Tax-advantaged cash value accumulation

Types of Universal Life:
- Indexed Universal Life (IUL): Cash value linked to market index performance
- Guaranteed Universal Life (GUL): Emphasis on death benefit with minimal cash value
- Variable Universal Life (VUL): Cash value invested in sub-accounts similar to mutual funds

Typical Coverage Amounts: $100,000 to $5,000,000

Best For:
- Those seeking flexibility in premiums and coverage
- Individuals with variable income
- Long-term coverage with investment component
- Estate planning with flexibility

4. VARIABLE LIFE INSURANCE

Variable life insurance allows policyholders to invest cash value in various investment options, similar to mutual funds.

Key Features:
- Cash value invested in sub-accounts (stocks, bonds, money markets)
- Death benefit and cash value fluctuate with investment performance
- Policyholder controls investment allocation
- Fixed premium payments required
- Higher potential returns with higher risk

Benefits:
- Potential for significant cash value growth
- Investment control and diversification
- Tax-deferred investment growth
- Professional investment management options
- Lifetime coverage

Risks:
- Cash value and death benefit can decrease with poor investment performance
- Market risk exposure
- Higher fees than other policy types
- Requires active management

Typical Coverage Amounts: $100,000 to $5,000,000

Best For:
- Experienced investors seeking growth potential
- Those comfortable with market risk
- Long-term investment and protection goals
- High income individuals

5. FINAL EXPENSE INSURANCE

Final expense insurance (burial insurance) is a small whole life policy designed to cover funeral costs and final expenses.

Key Features:
- Coverage amounts typically $5,000 to $35,000
- Guaranteed acceptance options available
- No medical exam required
- Fixed premiums
- Fast approval process

Benefits:
- Relieves family of funeral cost burden
- Easy qualification process
- Affordable premiums for seniors
- Immediate coverage in many cases
- Cash value accumulation

Average Funeral Costs Covered:
- Funeral service: $2,000 - $5,000
- Burial plot: $1,000 - $4,000
- Casket: $2,000 - $10,000
- Headstone: $1,000 - $3,000
- Other expenses: $1,000 - $3,000
Total Average: $7,000 - $25,000

Best For:
- Seniors aged 50-85
- Those with health issues
- Covering funeral and final expenses
- Leaving small inheritance for family

6. GROUP LIFE INSURANCE

Group life insurance is coverage provided through an employer or organization, typically term life insurance.

Key Features:
- Coverage through employer or membership organization
- Lower premiums due to group rates
- Limited underwriting or no medical exam
- Coverage often 1-3x annual salary
- Portable options may be available

Benefits:
- Affordable or free coverage through employer
- Easy enrollment during employment
- No medical exam in most cases
- Immediate coverage upon eligibility

Limitations:
- Coverage ends when employment ends
- Limited coverage amounts
- Not portable in many cases
- May require conversion to individual policy upon leaving

Typical Coverage Amounts: $10,000 to $500,000 (based on salary multiple)

Best For:
- Basic coverage through employment
- Supplementing individual policies
- Temporary coverage during career years
