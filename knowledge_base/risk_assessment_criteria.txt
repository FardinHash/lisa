LIFE INSURANCE RISK ASSESSMENT CRITERIA

HIGH-RISK HEALTH CONDITIONS

The following conditions are generally considered high-risk for life insurance underwriting:
- Active cancer or cancer diagnosis within the past 5 years
- Severe heart disease or recent heart attack
- Kidney disease requiring dialysis
- Liver disease or cirrhosis
- HIV/AIDS
- Terminal illness with limited life expectancy
- Severe uncontrolled diabetes
- Recent stroke or severe neurological disorders

MODERATE-RISK HEALTH CONDITIONS

The following conditions are considered moderate risk when properly managed:
- Well-controlled diabetes (Type 1 or Type 2)
- High blood pressure controlled with medication
- High cholesterol managed with medication
- Mild to moderate asthma
- Anxiety or depression (stable and treated)
- Sleep apnea with treatment
- Past cancer (5+ years in remission)

HIGH-RISK OCCUPATIONS

Occupations that may require specialized underwriting or higher premiums:
- Construction workers (especially high-rise work)
- Mining and offshore oil workers
- Commercial pilots and aircraft crew
- Firefighters and police officers
- Professional race car drivers
- Deep-sea divers
- Logging and forestry workers
- Military personnel in active combat zones

PREMIUM RATING FACTORS

Age-Based Risk Factors:
- Ages under 30: Lower risk, approximately 20% below standard rates
- Ages 30-39: Standard baseline risk
- Ages 40-49: Moderate increased risk, approximately 50% above baseline
- Ages 50-59: Significantly increased risk, approximately 2.5x baseline
- Ages 60+: High risk, approximately 4x baseline rates

Tobacco Use Impact:
- Tobacco users (cigarettes, cigars, chewing tobacco, vaping) typically pay 2.5 to 3 times higher premiums than non-smokers
- Must be tobacco-free for 12+ months to qualify for non-smoker rates

Health Rating Classifications:
- Preferred Plus/Elite: Best health profile, 20% below standard rates
- Preferred: Very good health, 10% below standard rates  
- Standard: Average health, baseline rates
- Substandard (Table ratings): Various health issues, 50%+ above standard rates

Term Length Impact:
- 10-year terms: Approximately 10% below standard annual rate
- 15-year terms: Standard baseline
- 20-year terms: Approximately 10% above standard
- 25-year terms: Approximately 20% above standard
- 30-year terms: Approximately 30% above standard

ELIGIBILITY AGE REQUIREMENTS

Standard Eligibility:
- Minimum age for individual policies: 18 years
- Maximum age for term life insurance: 75 years
- Maximum age for whole life insurance: 85 years
- No maximum age for guaranteed issue policies

Senior Considerations:
- Applicants over 65 seeking coverage above $1 million may face extensive underwriting
- Ages 75+ are typically limited to guaranteed issue, final expense, or simplified issue policies
